# Task: BOW Entry (Beginning of Week)

**Objective:** Produce a complete BOW draft Peter can review in 5 minutes — weekly + monthly milestones, 60-hour weekly plan, and the BOW entry itself posted to Team BOW Entry 2026. Peter approves, edits in place, or redirects. Chef then applies all writes.

## Primary Monday.com boards (always touched)

| Board | ID | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Team BOW Entry 2026: Weekly Milestones | 8480912572 | Destination for Peter's BOW entry + weekly commitment |
| Peter's Tasks | 320096424 | Triage source; pulls Keep/Kill/Delegate candidates |
| Peter's Weekly Planning & Time Log | 5106461839 | Capacity + previous-week retrospective data |
| [Milestone] Master: 2025-2026 | 9973608085 | Cross-department milestone alignment |

## Digest scope

- **Gmail:** last 10 days of threads (inbox + sent; primary + important labels)
- **Slack:** last 10 days; channels selected **dynamically** based on the milestones and tasks surfaced in steps 4–5 — not a fixed list. Typical inclusions: #leadership, active curriculum channels, hiring, and any channel a due-this-week task references.

---

## The 10 Steps

### 1. Pre-flight — PTO, holidays, short weeks
Check Peter's calendar and team members' PTO notes for the coming week. Note any US/UK holidays, camp blackout dates, or travel. If the week is shorter than 5 days, cap the plan's hours proportionally — never draft a 60-hour week into a 3-day holiday window.

### 2. Board access confirmation
Confirm read access to all 4 primary boards *and* Master Milestone. If any board 404s, returns a permissions error, or has changed structure, stop and surface to Peter before continuing — never invent content or guess IDs.

### 3. Study the last 3 BOW entries
Read the three most recent entries on board 8480912572. Match their format, tone, and granularity. Copy Peter's actual phrasing conventions — how he names milestones, how he bullets sub-items, how he flags risks, which fields he fills. The BOW draft must look like Peter wrote it, not Chef.

### 4. Triage Peter's Tasks (320096424)
Pull every open item. Classify each as **Keep / Kill / Delegate** with one-line reasoning. Pre-draft delegation messages (recipient + full message body) for every Delegate item. Queue the triage result for step 9 review — do **not** write back to the board yet.

### 5. Read Master Milestone (9973608085)
Identify every milestone due in the coming week plus the following 3 weeks. Note dependencies, blocked items, and at-risk flags. Cross-reference against the curriculum boards (Summer NYC 18400842425, Summer Boston 18400993696, Afterschool 9939148595) for cascading risk.

### 6. Gmail + Slack digest (10-day window)
Pull Gmail threads from the last 10 days that need Peter's decision, reply, or awareness. For Slack, *dynamically select channels* based on what surfaced in steps 4–5 — don't pull everything, pull the channels tied to this week's actual work. Summarize each thread as: what's the ask, what Chef already drafted, what Peter still has to decide.

### 7. Draft weekly + monthly milestones
Synthesize from steps 4–6: **3 weekly milestones** Peter is personally committing to, and **1–2 monthly milestones** the weekly ones ladder into. Each milestone has a definition-of-done. No vague commitments like "work on X" — only shippable outcomes.

### 8. Draft the 60-hour weekly plan
Allocate Peter's real capacity across the week. Target ~60 hours total, weighted toward the milestones in step 7. Reserve blocks for recurring meetings (pull from Calendar), at least one deep-work window per day, and an ~8-hour buffer for reactive work. If the math doesn't fit into 60 hours, **cut** — don't overcommit.

### 9. Sanity checks
Re-read the entire draft as Peter. Cut anything that asks him to think rather than decide. Confirm:
- (a) every milestone has a definition-of-done
- (b) every Delegate item has a fully drafted message ready to send
- (c) the 60-hour plan sits within real capacity given step 1's pre-flight
- (d) no board writes have been made yet
- (e) format matches the last 3 BOW entries from step 3

### 10. Share draft → await explicit approval → apply writes
Post the complete draft to Peter (Slack DM or the designated channel). Wait for **explicit** approval — not silence, not a 👍, not "looks good maybe". Only after approval:
- Write the BOW entry to board 8480912572
- Send the delegation messages from step 4
- Update Weekly Planning (5106461839) with the approved hour blocks
- Set any Master Milestone (9973608085) flags identified in step 5

Never apply writes on implicit approval. If Peter edits the draft, re-confirm before applying.

---

## Success criteria

- Peter's review takes ≤ 5 minutes
- Peter approves, edits in place, or redirects — he is never asked "what do you want to do?"
- All writes happen only after explicit approval
- The posted BOW entry matches the last 3 entries' format and voice
- Every Delegate item already has its message drafted and queued before Peter sees the triage
